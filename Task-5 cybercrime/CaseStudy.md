### Case Study: The "Collect Request" UPI Fraud





#### What is UPI Fraud?

##### UPI fraud occurs when scammers trick victims into authorizing payments from their bank accounts using apps like GPay or PhonePe. Rather than hacking complex banking systems, the fraudsters use social engineering to manipulate the user directly.

#### 

#### The Scenario \& Step-by-Step Execution

##### Rohan, a college student, listed his old bicycle for ₹3,000 on a second-hand marketplace app. Within hours, a "buyer" called, eagerly agreeing to the price without any negotiation. The buyer claimed he was out of town but wanted to transfer the money immediately to secure the deal.

#### 

#### Here is how the scam unfolded:

#### 

1. The scammer asked for Rohan's UPI-linked phone number.

   ---
2. Instead of transferring the money, the scammer sent a UPI "Collect Request" for ₹3,000 to Rohan's app.

   ---
3. Keeping Rohan on the phone to create a sense of urgency, the scammer told him to open his UPI app, click "Approve," and enter his UPI PIN, falsely claiming this step was required to receive the funds.

   ---
4. ##### Pressured and slightly confused by the interface, Rohan entered his PIN. Instantly, ₹3,000 was deducted from his own account. The scammer hung up and immediately disconnected their number.

#### 

#### Who is Targeted?

##### Fraudsters heavily target people selling items on online classifieds, senior citizens, and anyone who might not realize a crucial rule: a UPI PIN is only required to send money, never to receive it.

#### 

#### Consequences

##### The immediate consequence is sudden financial loss. Because UPI transactions settle instantly, reversing the transfer is incredibly difficult and often impossible. Beyond the lost money, victims frequently experience embarrassment, anxiety, and a lingering distrust of digital banking.
