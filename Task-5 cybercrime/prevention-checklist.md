#### **The "Stay Safe Online" Checklist for College Students**



##### College life means constantly juggling assignments, group chats, online shopping, and splitting bills. But it also makes us prime targets for cyber scams. Here are 8 straightforward habits to keep your data and your wallet safe:



#### **Protecting Your Money (UPI \& Banking)**



##### 1\. The Golden Rule of UPI: You never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If your app prompts you for a PIN, money is leaving your account. Always double-check the "To" name before hitting send.

##### 

##### 2\. Beware of "Customer Support" Screen Sharing: If a seller on OLX or a "bank executive" asks you to download apps like AnyDesk, TeamViewer, or screen-share on a WhatsApp video call to "help you out," hang up immediately. They just want to see your OTPs and PINs.

##### 

##### **Smart Browsing \& Social Media**

##### 

##### 3\. Don't Bank on Cafe or Campus Wi-Fi: Free Wi-Fi in the library or the local tapri is great for downloading PDFs, but it’s notoriously easy to snoop on. Always switch to your mobile data when making a payment or logging into your bank.

##### 

##### 4\. Spot Fake Internship Offers: If an HR recruiter for a "guaranteed work-from-home internship" asks you to pay a registration fee, laptop deposit, or training fee upfront, it’s a scam. Legitimate companies pay you, not the other way around.

##### 

##### 5\. Stop Oversharing in Real-Time: Don't post a picture of your concert ticket barcodes or your boarding passes. Also, avoid tagging your live location on Instagram stories while you are still sitting at that cafe—post it after you leave.

##### 

##### 6\. Ignore the "Electricity Bill" SMS: Scammers frequently send mass text messages claiming your electricity will be cut off tonight at 9 PM, or that your SIM card KYC is expiring, alongside a shady link. Never click links in unexpected text messages.



#### **Securing Your Devices**



##### 7\. Lock Your Tech in the Hostel: Never leave your laptop open and unattended in the library, lab, or even your hostel room if the door is open. Just hit Windows + L (or Cmd + Control + Q on Mac) whenever you walk away.

##### 

##### 8\. Stop Reusing Your ERP Password: Don't use the same password for your college student portal and your personal email or banking apps. If the university database gets compromised, your personal accounts go down with it.



#### **What to do if you get scammed?**



##### Don't panic and don't feel embarrassed—these scams are designed to trick smart people. Act fast:

##### 

##### Call the National Cybercrime Helpline: Dial 1930 immediately. The faster you call, the higher the chance they can freeze the fraudulent transaction.
